John Deere watch app

A troubleshooting app on Apple Watch for JohnDeere customers who are baby boomers.

My Role

Responsible for user interviews, product strategies and all the design deliverables.

Tools & Methods

  • Sketch, Invision
  • Contextual Inquiries, Competitive Analysis, Personas, Mockups, Prototyping, Usability testing

    • Goal

    Identify a pain point and design an Apple Watch app to solve it for John Deere baby boomer users.

    Our Process

    Milestone 1: Define use cases

    What we did

  • Analyzed a few categories that John Deere has products in, and went with the Turfs category since we're located in Bay Area and don't have as many farms.
  • Interviewed with John Deere customers and dealers and learnd about what pain points most customers experience and how they solve them.
  • Crafted 3 personas based on our data, and chose business owners as target users because they're more likely to use an Apple Watch.
  • Based on the data collected from interviews and our persona, we came up with three use cases for the app - Overview, Troubleshooting and Performance.

    • What we learned

    It was extremely challenging when we first started, because baby boommers are usually not avid users of Apple Watch, and John Deere has a smaller customer base in Bay Area, which makes it difficult to find users to talk to in person.

  • We used both Qualitative and Quantative methods during our research. We interviewed customers and dealers as much as we could in person, and then we also did research online to understand customers' behaviors.
  • We learned to narrow down to only one product category after learning more about the company and considering our geographics constraint.
  • We didn't base our persona on our actual interviewees because average baby boomers might not need or be able to afford an apple watch. Therefore, we created a new persona based on collected data and our constraint.

    • Milestone 2: First design iteration & Design Review in class

    What we did

  • Developed the first version of the design for the watch app and presented in class.
  • The mockups below demonstrated the overview of machines, performance and troubleshooting use cases.
  • Created 3 versions of notifications for this app and we chose the transparent one.

    • What we learned

  • There were too many words (the Problem and Tips section) in the app, which doesn't align with the purpose of an apple watch.
  • Too many different colors and the patterns were not consistent, which could be confusing to users.
  • It felt like the app was missing a landing page that points out the most important information in the app or helps users navigate.

    • Milestone 3: Second design iteration & Usability testing

    What we did

  • Created a menu page as the landing page to help users better understand how to navigate in the app.
  • Replaced one green color with white for the content of the app.
  • Reduced the amount of words in the Troubleshooting use case using icons.
  • Created a new manual section for users to look up whenever they need since we added more icons
  • Conducted usability testing with 7+ users, including both business and individual owners.

    • What we learned

  • The way business owners manage their machines was different than we expected. It's more common business owners hire a contractor to manage the project and the superintendents who use the machines. Therefore, most business owners don't personally manage the machines at all.
  • Sometimes individual owners prefer to try to fix the machines themselves first.
  • We felt like the manual section might not align with the purpose of Apple Watch, because most users don't use it to acquire a large amount of knowledge.
  • Users mentioned the color scheme was confusing and not clean during interviews.

    • Milestone 4: Last design iteration & Usability testing

    What we did

  • Went back to using individual owners as our target users after understanding business owners' behaviors during usability testing.
  • Decided to focus on the Troubleshooting use case (both preventive and reactive) and notifications after modifying our persona and given the time constraint.
  • Created 3 rapid prototypes and ran testing with 5+ users.
  • Used the color orange yellow for the Schedule Now button to encourage users to schedule appointments with Dealers
  • We provided different caller options for different scenarios - Schedule a maintenance appointment with the dealer vs. Contact the technician to fix a part.
  • We provided both Tutorial and Contact Technician options for the troublshooting option based on the feedback from usability testing.

    • What we learned

  • The most important thing we learned in this iteration is how to use Rapid Prototyping properly and avoid the Sunk Cost fallacy. We created more than 3 prototypes over the course of 2 days, and each of them was significantly different than the previous one because we iterated based on the feedback from our usability testing each time.
  • It might be good to demonstrate the hands-off approach with the iPhone for the self troubleshooting use case.
  • The current landing page (the menu) can be improved in order to align with the Apple Watch design.

    • Takeaways

    Key Learnings

  • It's critical to validate our hypothesis on our target users early on in the process before we even started with the design process.
  • We've become to understand the importance of the exposure to the Apple Watch device during the process because that helped us refine our use cases more efficiently.
  • Given the geographics challenge, we learned how to conduct usability testing with different groups that might not have all the characterestics of the target users, because it was still helpful for us to identify fundamental UX/UI issues.
  • The biggest takeway for me is how to design a product that solves an actual problem for users and aligns with the purpose of the device to provide a better user expereince.

    • Next Steps

    Personally I plan to spend more time and work on this app as a side project after the class. Apple Watch is mainly for notifications, and it feels like our app is still trying to do too much. Therefore, I plan to use more apple watch apps to be further familiar with the the expereince of the device, modify the design of our troubleshooting app, create a few more prototypes and run usability testing so that the app will really be able to solve a problem for users and align with the purpose of a device.
