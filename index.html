Apple Watch app

DESCRIPTION

A product design project I worked on in a team of 5 for my Human Computer Interaction class. I was responsible for making the mockups, prototyping and running usability interviews.

THE GOAL

The goal of the project is to design an Apple Watch app for Baby Boomers that use John Deere products.

THE CHALLENGE

  • The demographics challenge is that Baby Boomers (target users) not as tech savvy and might not be avid users of the Apple Watch device.
  • The geographic challenge is that we are located in bay area and there are not many John Deere users since most people don’t have a lawn for their house and we don’t have many farms here.
  • Most of the team members don’t have an apple watch, and therefore, the team is not familiar with the apple watch’s user experience and information architecture

    • RESULTS

    What I learn the most through this project is that it’s important to think of the device when I design a new product or feature. The information structure has to make sense in the context and aligns with the purpose of the design. It’s critical not to be afraid of sunk cost. It was difficult to let go of something I spent hours on but it was helpful when I was willing to start over and apply the feedback I collected into the new prototype.

    SKILLS & TOOLS

    Competitive Analysis, Contextual Inquiries, Persona, Storyboarding, URD, Mockups, Prototyping, Usability Interviews, Sketch app

    I am still working on updating the prototype based on the feedback we collected from the usability interviews. More details on the design process and the final design.

    See current prototype on Invision

    Game of Votes

    DESCRIPTION

    A board game I created as the final project for my Game Design Fundamentals class.

    THE GOAL

    The objective of the project is to create a non digital game for this class and it will be graded on the rulebook in terms of how easy to understand the rules, the design of the game, the analysis in terms of what and why you made certain changes, and the fun of the game. This is the first time I took a game design class and I have never had any exposure in this field.

    RESULTS

    A board game for 4 players and they represent the candidates in the 2016 US Presidential Election. As the election is getting closer and closer, all candidates have to fight for every single vote they can get in every state. Sometimes they might be at crossroads, facing the decision on whether they should run a clean or dirty campaign. Now, you are representing your candidate in this election, and you have a chance to win this election if you play your game right.

    BridgeTech

    DESCRIPTION

    This is a team project I worked on in my Product Validation and Definition class at CMU. After the class, I decided to continue this project and finish creating the mockups.

    THE GOAL

    The goal of the project is to identify underserved needs in the recruiting and HR management field, come up with a solution, understand the product-market fit and suggest features for the potential solution.

    PROBLEM

    Startups frequently hire contractors because of their limited resources and temporary needs for different roles. However, companies find it difficult to work with contractors efficiently even with the help of existing contractor management tools. Eventually, startups not only fail to save money by hiring contractors, but they also might have trouble completing the project and delivering to users on time.

    SOLUTION

    BridgeTech is a mobile app that helps startups to manage contractors more effectively by tackling the issues identified during the interview process. BridgeTech allows companies to focus on the real issues and build their own products by managing all these tedious processes.

    SKILLS & TOOLS

    Contextual Inquiries, Competitive Analysis, Persona, Storyboarding, URD, Mockups, Sketch app

    More details to come on the process and mockups.

    AngelHunt

    DESCRIPTION

    This is my final project for the class Introduction to Meteor.js on Coursera.

    THE GOAL

    The objective of the project is to create a website that allows users to add their favorite websites to the platform and let others upvote or downvote them.

    RESULTS

    Similar to how Reddit or ProductHunt works, when a user visits the website, he or she will need to create an account or sign in in order to add a website or post a comment. User will see a list of websites that have been added by previous visitors, the ranking of the sites and how many upvotes or downvotes each site has. User can click the arrow icons to upvote or downvote a site on AngelHunt. If user clicks "Detail", it will take him or her to the detail page where the user can see comments on a specific site.

    SKILLS & TOOLS

    Web Development, Responsive Web Design, Meteor.js, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, jQuery, MongoDB

    See project on Github
